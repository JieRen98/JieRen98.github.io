I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at [King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST)](https://www.kaust.edu.sa/), advised by [Prof. David E. Keyes](https://www.kaust.edu.sa/en/study/faculty/david-keyes). I received my B.Eng. in Electronics Engineering from Xidian University, China, in 2021.

My research focuses on rethinking classical numerical algorithms for modern heterogeneous architectures. I study the co-design of numerical methods — including sparse factorizations and finite element discretizations — alongside algorithmic scheduling and GPU architecture, with the goal of exposing and exploiting mathematical structure under extreme parallelism. Rather than treating hardware constraints as obstacles, I use them as a lens to investigate how sparsity, numerical stability, and task granularity must be reformulated when traditional CPU-centric assumptions no longer hold. This perspective has led to contributions spanning scientific computing and machine learning systems, with publications at SC, NeurIPS, ICML, ICS, and ECCV, and recognition as a **Gordon Bell Prize Finalist at SC'24**.

I have deep expertise in NVIDIA GPU architecture and have made open-source contributions to projects including [CUTLASS](https://github.com/NVIDIA/cutlass/tree/main/examples/65_distributed_gemm) and PyTorch. I have held research positions at **NVIDIA Research**, **ETH Zürich** (HiPEAC Grant), **Baidu** (ERNIE Star), **University of Edinburgh**, and **MEGVII Inc.**
